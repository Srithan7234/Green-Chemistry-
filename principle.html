12 Principles of Green Chemistry

Green chemistry is a science-driven approach that promotes the design of products and processes that minimize the use and generation of hazardous substances. It aims to reduce environmental impact while maintaining efficiency and sustainability.

The 12 Principles

  1. Prevent Waste: Reducing waste generation is more efficient and environmentally friendly than treating waste afterward.
  2. Maximize Atom Economy: Processes should optimize the use of all raw materials, minimizing waste generation.
  3. Design Less Hazardous Chemical Syntheses: Methods should use safer substances to protect human health and the environment.
  4. Design Safer Chemicals: Chemical products should function effectively while having minimal toxicity.
  5. Use Safer Solvents and Reaction Conditions: Reactions should minimize the use of harmful solvents or auxiliary substances.
  6. Increase Energy Efficiency: Processes should be designed to minimize energy use, operating at room temperature and pressure when possible.
  7. Use Renewable Feedstocks: Whenever possible, renewable rather than non-renewable materials should be used.
  8. Reduce Derivatives: Unnecessary modifications like protecting groups should be avoided to minimize resource use.
  9. Catalysis: Catalysts increase efficiency by enabling reactions to proceed with lower energy and waste.
  10. Design for Degradation: Chemical products should break down into non-harmful substances after use.
  11. Real-time Analysis for Pollution Prevention: Processes should include real-time monitoring to prevent hazardous by-products.
  12. Inherently Safer Chemistry for Accident Prevention: Chemicals and processes should be designed to minimize risks such as explosions or leaks.
